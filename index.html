About the Artist
Bio portrait

Ruganintwali
Patrick

Kirehe, Rwanda

Born in Kigali, Rwanda in 1983, Patrick RUGANINTWALI draws inspiration from his daily life, those around him, his encounters with other artists, and his travels.

Self-taught and drawn after his secondary studies to sculpture and painting, his taste quickly led him to mix different materials (wood, sand, fabric, pebbles), which defined his style.

Umugabiro w'Inzira

2021 · Mixed natural pigments on board

A large-format spiral composition inspired by the winding paths of the Akagera River. The piece uses seven nested coils, each representing a generation of the Gisaka kingdom's rulers. The deep ochre tones were sourced from Bugesera volcanic soil.

02

Ikirere cy'Amahoro

2022 · Cow dung & kaolin on board

"The Sky of Peace." Created during a residency following the commemoration season, this piece uses only black and white — the most traditional palette — to speak about silence, remembrance, and resilience. The repeating diamond grid is an old symbol of protection.

03

Inzira y'Ejo

2023 · Natural pigments, large-scale triptych

"The Road of Tomorrow." A triptych commission for the Kigali Convention Centre, measuring 3 × 1.5 metres each panel. Patrick fused traditional coil patterns with bold triangular forms reflecting Rwanda's architectural growth — the ancient in conversation with the new.
